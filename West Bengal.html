West Bengal

Howrah Bridge
The Howrah Bridge, also known as Rabindra Setu, is famous as an iconic landmark and a symbol of Kolkata, connecting Howrah and Kolkata across the Hooghly River.

Victoria Memorial
Representing the resplendent and majestic Italian Renaissance style of architecture, beautifully blended with Indian influences, Victoria Memorial Hall stands today, as a veritable icon of the city of Kolkata.

Darjeeling
Darjeeling is famous for its world-renowned tea, the scenic views of the Kanchenjunga mountain range, the historic Darjeeling Himalayan Railway (Toy Train), and its rich cultural and natural heritage.

Sunderban National Park
The Sundarbans is of universal importance for globally endangered species including the Royal Bengal Tiger, Ganges and Irawadi dolphins, estuarine crocodiles and the critically endangered endemic river terrapin (Batagur baska).

Durgapur
Burdwan is home to Durgapur, the 'Steel City' of West Bengal. One of India's few planned cities, its success is largely attributable to architects Benjamin Polk and Allen Stein.

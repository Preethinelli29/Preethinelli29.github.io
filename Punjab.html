PUNJAB

Golden Temple
the Golden Temple stands as a beacon of spirituality, unity, and service. Floating like a shimmering mirage of gold on the tranquil waters of Amrit Sarovar, the temple is a place of worship.

Jallianwala Bagh
The Jallianwala Bagh massacre, also known as the Amritsar massacre, took place on 13 April 1919. A large crowd had gathered at the Jallianwala Bagh in Amritsar, Punjab, British India, during the annual Baisakhi fair to protest against the Rowlatt Act.

Jalandhar
Jalandhar is famous for its sports goods industry, particularly known as the "Sports Hub" of India, and is also recognized for its historical significance, cultural heritage, and vibrant markets.

Patiala
Known as the 'City of Palaces, Forts, and Gardens,' Patiala still retains its old-world charm. Founded by Baba Ala Singh, the visionary who established the Phulkian State, Patiala's name itself echoes its royal lineage.

Pathankot
Pathankot is famous for being the meeting point of three states (Punjab, Jammu & Kashmir, and Himachal Pradesh), its scenic location, cultural heritage, and the Ranjit Sagar Dam, a major tourist attraction.
